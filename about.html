About AMH Catalyst Center

Funding women creates positive ripple effects across our communities and our ecosystems.

We build the infrastructure that gets women funded and they do the rest.

Our Vision

We envision a world where women founders can access the capital they need to build, and capital systems evolve to recognize their talent and potential.

Our Why

Women build businesses that create positive ripple effects across our communities and ecosystems. They hire more women, invest in families, build for communities, and a more sustainable future. And yet, they remain incredibly underfunded.

We believe that when more women have access to capital, everyone benefits. AMH exists to level the playing field, so the future is shaped by the best ideas, not by limited access to capital. We owe it to our daughters, to this generation, and the next, to Amplify Markets for Her.

Our Story

AMH was built to make women founders visible, credible, and backable.

Some people stumble into purpose. Jenny Flores stumbled into a broken system and decided to build a better one. Born in El Salvador, Jenny immigrated to San Francisco at three years old. By the time she was in elementary school, she was translating for her mother and her community, navigating systems that weren’t designed for people like them, and finding the pathways through anyway.

That early experience of bridging worlds, of making the opaque legible, became the foundation of everything she would build. Across a twenty-year career spanning corporate, public, and nonprofit sectors, Jenny became an architect of systems that moved capital where it was needed most.

At Wells Fargo, she co-designed a $420M fund, a landmark initiative that supported 336,000 small businesses, 51% of them women-owned, and preserved 461,000 jobs during the COVID-19 pandemic. She’s led multi-million dollar investments and a billion dollar fund targeting growth-oriented entrepreneurs across the US.

Today she’s building for the future. Jenny sees the blind spots in venture capital and is creating the tools and pathways to make women founders more visible, credible, and backable. Co-founded with Grace Lee, AMH stands for Amplifying Markets for Her, a dedication and a declaration: the market for women-founded ventures is far larger than the capital flowing into it, and AMH exists to close that gap.

Women are a smart investment, and the data proves it. They deliver strong returns, hire more women, and build businesses that strengthen communities and support the environment. Investing in women doesn’t just drive financial outcome, it drives broader impact.

Join us in rewriting the playbook for venture funding and investing in a better future for all.
